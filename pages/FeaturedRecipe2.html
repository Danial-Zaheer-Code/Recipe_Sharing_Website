Beef Wellington

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lb beef tenderloin, trimmed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 8 oz cremini mushrooms, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup dry white wine
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • 6 slices prosciutto
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Beef:
    • Preheat your oven to 425째F (220째C).
    • Season the beef tenderloin with salt and black pepper. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over high heat. Sear the beef on all sides until browned. Remove from heat and brush with Dijon mustard. Let it cool.
  2. Prepare the Duxelles:
    • In the same skillet, melt butter over medium heat. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add finely chopped mushrooms and cook until the mixture is dry and browned, about 10 minutes. Add white wine and cook until the liquid evaporates. Let it cool.
  3. Assemble the Wellington:
    • Lay out prosciutto slices on a sheet of plastic wrap, overlapping slightly. Spread the mushroom duxelles over the prosciutto. Place the beef on top and roll it up tightly using the plastic wrap. Chill for 15 minutes.
    • Roll out the puff pastry on a floured surface. Remove the beef from the plastic wrap and place it in the center of the pastry. Fold the pastry around the beef and seal the edges. Brush with beaten egg.
  4. Bake the Wellington:
    • Place the wrapped beef on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the internal temperature of the beef reaches 130째F (54째C) for medium-rare.
  5. Rest and Serve:
    • Let the Wellington rest for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve with your favorite sides.
